# Product content methodology

Short-form, product-led content. Landing-page copy, product descriptions, social posts, newsletters, transactional emails. Different rules than long-form articles — voice matters more, length matters less, every word does multiple jobs.

## When to apply

Use this reference when the register is `short`:

- Social posts (LinkedIn, X, threads, Bluesky)
- Newsletter copy
- Product descriptions and feature pages
- Landing page section copy
- Transactional emails
- Push notifications

Long-form articles, even when product-related, use `article.md`.

## What changes vs long-form

| Dimension | Long-form | Product / short |
|---|---|---|
| Voice match | Important | **Paramount** — the whole piece IS the voice |
| Specificity | Required throughout | Required, but compressed |
| Sources / citations | 4+ inline links | 0-2; reserve for high-stakes claims |
| Length | 1,500-4,000 words | 30-500 words |
| Structure (FAQ, TOC, schema) | Required | Usually skipped |
| AEO patterns | Required | Adapted: short-form has its own extraction patterns |
| Humanization | Standard | **Heightened** — short copy with AI patterns is more visible |

## Short-form voice rules

The voice profile applies, but the surface area is smaller. Tactical adjustments:

- **Open with the strongest sentence.** Long-form can warm up; short-form can't. The first sentence has to land.
- **Every word does multiple jobs.** Setting tone, conveying fact, advancing the call to action.
- **Cut filler aggressively.** "We're excited to announce..." → just announce.
- **One thought per piece.** Long-form covers a topic; short-form makes a point.

## The opening line bar

In long-form, the bar for the opening is "specific factual statement." In short-form, the bar is the same plus:

- Could this open a longer article? If yes, it's *too generic* for short-form.
- Does it set the voice immediately? Within 5 words.
- Does it imply the rest of the piece, so the reader is already curious?

Examples:

| Bad | Good |
|---|---|
| We're excited to share our latest update. | The TOC engine just got 4× faster. Here is what changed. |
| Our team has been working hard on this. | This took 3 attempts, and the third one was the right one. |
| Here are the top 5 things to know about onboarding. | Onboarding is broken when the first call is a demo. Here is what works instead. |

## Length discipline

| Channel | Target length |
|---|---|
| Tweet / X post | 270 chars (under 280 limit, leaves room for a link) |
| Bluesky | 280 chars (leaves room for a link) |
| LinkedIn post (single) | 150-300 words |
| LinkedIn carousel slide | 25-50 words per slide |
| Threads post (single) | 280-500 chars |
| Newsletter (top-of-fold) | 80-150 words |
| Newsletter (full) | 300-600 words |
| Product description | 60-180 words |
| Landing page hero subtitle | 15-30 words |
| Push notification | 60-110 chars |
| Transactional email body | 80-200 words |

Don't pad to fill the channel maximum. The shortest version that conveys the point is the right one.

## Product-specific rules

When the content is *about* a product:

### Lead with the user value, not the feature

| Feature-led | Value-led |
|---|---|
| Our new dashboard has 14 widgets including a real-time activity feed. | See exactly what your team shipped this week, without asking. |
| The API now supports webhooks. | Get notified the moment a customer's status changes — no polling. |
| Pricing tiers updated to include 10 seats. | Bring your whole team without a per-seat haggle. |

Feature-led copy is for engineers reading the changelog. Value-led copy is for buyers deciding whether to try the product.

### Specifics, not superlatives

- "4× faster" beats "much faster"
- "$3.96 per article" beats "affordable per-article pricing"
- "80 articles a month" beats "generous monthly limit"

Numbers are credibility. Generic positives are AI cream.

### One CTA per piece

A LinkedIn post asking the reader to (a) star the repo (b) sign up for the beta (c) reply with thoughts (d) share with a friend is asking too much. Pick the highest-priority CTA and lead with it.

## Anti-patterns specific to short-form

In long-form, the AI-cream patterns are spread across enough text that some go unnoticed. Short-form makes them glaring. Watch for:

- **Faux excitement openers**: "Excited to share...", "Thrilled to announce...", "Game-changing news..."
- **Empty hashtags**: #content #marketing #AI as filler. Use no hashtags or use specific ones.
- **Cheesy emoji density**: 1-2 emoji at most, only if the brand voice uses them. Most brand voices don't.
- **"Let me tell you a story"**: actually telling a story is great; signposting that you're about to is AI.
- **The 3-bullet inside the 200-word post**: short-form rarely needs nested structure.

## Newsletter rhythm

Newsletters live or die on the open. The subject + preheader is the conversion event; the body is the payoff.

| Element | Target |
|---|---|
| Subject line | 30-55 chars; specific, not clickbait |
| Preheader | 60-90 chars; continues or completes the subject |
| Top section | 80-150 words, makes the case for reading on |
| Body | 300-600 words, one thought |
| One CTA | links to the long-form or product or signup |

Subject line is a separate rhetorical problem. Best opener for a newsletter is rarely the best subject line.

## Landing page copy

Hero sections live and die on the headline. Same rules as long-form first sentence, plus:

- Headline pairs with subtitle: headline carries the claim, subtitle qualifies and specifies
- Visual hero (image / video / preview) complements the words, doesn't substitute for them
- The CTA button is part of the copy, not an afterthought ("Start free" beats "Get started" beats "Click here")

Below the hero, each section is short-form: one idea, lead-with-value, specific over superlative.

## Audit checklist for short-form

- [ ] Opening line specific and voice-consistent
- [ ] Length appropriate for the channel
- [ ] One thought, not three
- [ ] No filler openers ("Excited to...", "We're thrilled to...")
- [ ] Specifics, not superlatives
- [ ] One CTA, clearly stated
- [ ] Voice profile fields visibly applied (tone, sentence rhythm, characteristic phrases)
- [ ] No banned phrases
- [ ] No empty hashtags or filler emojis
- [ ] Headline + subtitle pair (for landing copy)
